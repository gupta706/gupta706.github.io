Mobility is being reshaped by shared rides, connectivity, and electrification —
but the industry is fragmented, and seamless door-to-door service requires many
operators to cooperate. We study the **business models and pricing mechanisms**
that make that cooperation profitable and fair.

Our frameworks derive fair prices in ridehailing systems with asymmetric demand
and travel time, and design incentive and profit-sharing schemes so that
independent operators in a multimodal network are better off participating than
going it alone. We also study rebalancing — moving idle vehicles to where demand
will be — as a reinforcement learning problem.

### Highlights

- Fair pricing of ridehailing services under asymmetric demand and travel time.
- Incentive design and profit sharing across multimodal transportation networks.
- Multi-objective vehicle rebalancing via reinforcement learning.
